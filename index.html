A Hopfield network is a tiny brain made of twelve neurons. Each one is either leaning "on" or "off," and they constantly influence each other through weighted connections.

Here, each neuron is a pitch. The weight between two neurons is the consonance of the interval between them — a perfect fifth attracts strongly, a minor second repels.

E = −½ ΣΣ Wij si sj

At every tick, one neuron looks at its neighbours and nudges itself toward whatever value lowers E. Low energy literally means a chord — the network relaxes into attractor states that happen to be consonant.

Grab the square and wiggle. Where you wiggle (x) picks which pitches to shake; how wildly you wiggle (y, velocity) decides how hard. A big enough shake knocks the network out of one chord and into a new one.

Tap a node for its pitch. Tap a line for its interval and weight.
